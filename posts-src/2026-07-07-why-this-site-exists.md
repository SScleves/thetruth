# Why this site exists

<!-- Santi: this is your seed post, assembled from your own opening lines. Edit or delete freely. -->

Most people don't search. They inherit a story — from religion, culture, education — and it works
well enough. For most people, that's enough.

Some people can't stop noticing that the pieces don't fit.

This site is for the second group. It holds a book, *Something Doesn't Fit*, and a slow series of
discussions about the things the accepted frame handles badly: the stones that are older than the
timeline, the myths that repeat across cultures that never met, the physics that keeps describing
a system rather than a substance.

The rules here are simple.

- No claim without its strongest counterargument.
- Patterns over anecdotes — one anomaly can be dismissed; a shape that repeats is data.
- You're not asked to believe anything. You're asked whether you're willing to look.

— — —

New essays land on the front page as they're finished. The backlog is public — if you want to see
what's coming, it's in the open, next to the source of this entire site.
