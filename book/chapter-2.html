Part 1

Let’s break down what you just read.

Not as a story.

As a pattern.

Because stories can be dismissed.

They can be called myth, coincidence, exaggeration.

Patterns are harder to ignore.

They don’t ask you to believe anything.

They only ask you to notice what repeats.

So don’t focus on whether each piece is true in isolation.

Look at how the same structure appears —

across cultures that never met,

across time periods that never touched,

across domains that should have nothing in common.

This is not about proving a theory.

It’s about asking a simpler question:

Why does the same shape keep appearing?
